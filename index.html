Economic Impacts of a Performing Arts Center

A thriving performing arts center generates multiple layers of economic value. Below are several of them:

Job Creation & Local Employment

  • Direct jobs: performers, stage crew, ushers, ticketing, box office staff, technical staff.
  • Continuous roles: venue managers, marketing, administration, maintenance.
  • Indirect jobs: catering, food vendors, retailers, hotels, transportation, cleaning services.

When a performing arts center is active—hosting regular shows, concerts, community events—it sustains continuous employment rather than just seasonal gigs.

Boost to Local Businesses & Services

When audiences attend performances or events, they often spend money beyond just ticket prices:

  • Dining before/after shows in restaurants, cafes, bars
  • Parking, public transport, rideshares
  • Shopping local stores—event-related or incidental spending
  • Lodging/hotels if out-of-town performers or attendees stay overnight

This spending boosts revenue for nearby businesses. Studies show audience members spend significantly beyond just ticket admission per event.

Tourism, Visitor Spending & Multiplier Effects

A well-known performing arts center draws visitors from outside the immediate community. These might be people attending festivals, concerts, or touring shows.

These visitors spend on lodging, food, shopping, and other attractions, and this generates ripple effects across the local economy. For example, restaurants get more business, hire more staff, and that money stays circulating in the community.

This is known as the multiplier effect: money spent at the venue leads to more money flowing through the local economy.

Real Estate, Area Development & Urban Revitalization

Performing arts centers often act as anchor institutions that spark investment and growth in nearby areas.

  • Real estate values increase as cultural activity attracts residents and businesses.
  • Surrounding areas may see infrastructure upgrades like roads, lighting, and public safety.
  • Underused buildings may be transformed into galleries, studios, or cafes.

In Brooklyn, performing arts centers can play a major role in revitalizing neighborhoods through culture.

Performing Arts Theater vs Performing Arts Center: What’s the Difference?

The terms performing arts theater and performing arts center are often used interchangeably, but there are important differences.

  • Performing Arts Theater: Typically a single-purpose space designed specifically for plays, concerts, dance, or opera. It often includes fixed seating and a permanent stage.
  • Performing Arts Center: A multi-use facility that may include one or more theaters, rehearsal spaces, classrooms, galleries, or event spaces. These centers focus on both performance and community engagement.

Social and Cultural Impacts

Beyond economics, performing arts centers provide essential social and cultural value to the communities they serve. They promote inclusivity, identity, education, and emotional connection.

Cultural Enrichment & Identity

Performing arts centers help preserve and showcase local culture—through music, theater, storytelling, and dance. They give a voice to underrepresented communities and support local artists.

These venues foster pride, belonging, and a sense of identity. They give the community a shared cultural voice.

Education, Outreach & Talent Development

Many performing arts centers run workshops, school programs, and masterclasses that teach skills to youth and adults.

  • Builds future generations of artists, musicians, and theater makers
  • Improves school engagement and creative thinking
  • Strengthens self-confidence and communication abilities

These programs are especially important in underserved neighborhoods where students may have fewer creative outlets.

Community Events, Social Cohesion & Well‑Being

Performing arts centers offer a shared space for local celebrations, storytelling, and cultural exchange.

  • Events like open mic nights and community theater strengthen social bonds
  • Art has mental health benefits—reduces stress, builds empathy
  • Gatherings reduce isolation and increase connectedness

These centers serve as social glue—bringing people together across different backgrounds.

Inclusivity, Diversity & Accessibility

A well-designed performing arts center offers programs and pricing that are inclusive to all.

  • Low-cost or free events for underserved communities
  • Wheelchair-accessible seating and assistive listening options
  • Programming that reflects many cultures, languages, and identities

Accessibility isn’t just physical—it’s also cultural and economic.
